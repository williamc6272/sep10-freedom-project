Forensic Science

Forensic science uses scientific and technological tools to investigate crimes and analyze evidence. Advances in technology such as DNA profiling, digital forensics, and improved imaging have made investigations faster and more accurate. As new innovations like AI and advanced genetics emerge, the future of forensic science promises even greater precision and new ways to solve complex crimes. Despite these advancements, the high cost of forensic technologies often limits accessibility, with many tools costing tens of thousands of dollars. One huge problem is technology is inaccessible to people, and some forensic science labs dont recieve enough money. That brings the quesion, What technologies can help improve modern forensic science? Sustainable and scalable forensic technologies can improve case resolution rates, reduce backlogs in forensic labs.

There are many existing forensic science technologies that have great use but vary in prices. But as we know every technology has its own use in the forensics field.

OsetoID is a free, web based tool designed for forensic anthropologists, archaeologists, and students to identify unknown skeletal remains. This app also helps identify a bone if it is non-human, to which animal species does it belong. This app is free to use for everyone on Windows, IOS and more. Nano Tech Robots


One problem in the forensic world is the absense of evidence in a crime scence do to tamperment/destruction of the crime scene. In the future, microscopic forensic robots could be used to repair or reconstruct damaged evidence at a crime scene. These tiny machines would detect and follow specific frequencies or signal patterns, allowing them to locate broken particles of materials like fabric fibers, fingerprints, or DNA fragments. Reorganizing these particles into their original structure, the robots could help restore evidence that would otherwise be impossible to analyze. This technology would greatly improve accuracy in investigations and help solve cases that currently lack usable evidence. The microscopic forensic robots could be made from inexpensive materials like conductive components making each unit affordable to produce. They would also use low-energy systems, such as tiny rechargeable micro batteries or energy harvested from environmental frequencies, which reduces operating costs. This can help forensic scientists have a bigger budget to buy other stuff as well as being able to rapidly solve crime.
